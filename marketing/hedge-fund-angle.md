# The "hedge-fund, without the fund" angle

Your instinct — *take something that always seemed out of league for the average
person and put it in arm's reach* — is a great one. This file makes it work without
blowing up. Read the one warning, then use the reframe and the copy.

---

## The one warning (don't skip)

The tempting version is: *"Hedge funds make $3.66M/month — you can earn that too."*
**Don't ship that.** Two reasons:

1. **It's an implied return promise.** That's the exact claim that reclassifies
   non-custodial software as an investment offering. See `compliance-guardrails.md`.
2. **The number invites ridicule.** $3.66M/month is a *fund's* figure — an entity
   running billions of dollars of other people's money. Map it onto someone's
   $50–$10k pod and a crypto-native audience will do the math in two seconds and call
   you a hype merchant. You'd lose the credibility that is your entire edge.

**The fix: don't compete on returns. Compete on ACCESS.** Hedge funds never beat you
with better math — they beat you with a locked door. The door is real, people resent
it, and you actually remove it. That's the honest, stronger version of your idea.

## The reframe

> For decades, this *class* of strategy had a velvet rope: a million-dollar minimum,
> an "accredited investors only" sign, 2-and-20 fees, lock-ups, and — if you got in —
> a quarterly PDF. Vältgeist runs that class of machinery on *your own wallet*. No
> rope. No minimum. No cut of your money. You hold the keys.

Notice what this does and doesn't say. It **doesn't** promise you'll *earn* what a
hedge fund earns. It **does** say you now have *access* to the kind of tooling that
was gatekept — which is true, verifiable, and hits the exact "out of league → in
reach" nerve you wanted.

## The safe way to use hedge-fund *facts*

These are factual barriers, not return claims — safe to cite (keep them roughly
accurate; verify before a big campaign):

- **Fee model:** the classic "2 and 20" — ~2% annual management fee + 20% performance
  fee. Great contrast against your flat fee.
- **Accreditation:** US private funds are generally limited to accredited investors
  (~$1M net worth excluding home, or $200k+ income). A real, resented gate.
- **Minimums & lock-ups:** six-to-seven-figure minimums and redemption gates are
  standard. Contrast with "withdraw anytime, your keys."

**If you ever want a big aspirational number, make it a *market* fact, not a personal
one.** e.g. "Solana LPs earn $X in fees every day" — a true statement about the
opportunity, with no promise attached to any individual. But **source a real,
current figure** before you use it; don't invent one, and never phrase it as "you
will earn."

## Do / don't for this specific angle

| ❌ Don't | ✅ Do |
|---------|------|
| "Earn what a hedge fund earns" | "Access the class of tooling hedge funds gatekept" |
| "$3.66M/month — you can too" | "No minimum, no accreditation, no 2-and-20, no lock-up" |
| Any figure attached to *your* pod | Figures about *their fees and barriers* |
| "As good as the pros" (implies returns) | "The pros' machinery, on your wallet, on your terms" |

The feeling you're chasing — *this was never for people like me, and now it is* — is
fully delivered by the access story. You don't need the number. The number is the
part that gets you in trouble; the open door is the part that sells.
