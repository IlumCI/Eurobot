# Compliance guardrails for marketing copy

Read this once. It's not legal advice — it's a set of plain-language rules that keep
your marketing out of the two ditches that sink financial products: **promising
returns** and **sounding like you take custody / manage money**. Following these also
happens to make you *more* credible, not less.

> If you remember one thing: **describe the software, never the outcome.** You sell a
> tool people run on their own wallet. You do not sell returns, and you do not hold
> anyone's money. Keep every sentence on the right side of that line.

---

## The hard NOs (never say these)

- ❌ "Guaranteed / risk-free / no-loss / safe returns"
- ❌ "Passive income", "earn X% APY", any specific or implied return number
- ❌ "Invest with us", "deposit and earn", "grow your money"
- ❌ "We manage your funds / portfolio / capital"
- ❌ "Our fund", "assets under management", "we trade for you"
- ❌ "You can't lose", "downside protected", "we've got your back on losses"
- ❌ Backtest or demo numbers presented as expected future results
- ❌ Anything that positions the token as an investment that will go up in value

Each of these can pull you toward being treated as an investment offering, an
unlicensed adviser, or a custodial financial institution. All bad. All avoidable.

---

## The DOs (say it this way instead)

- ✅ "Non-custodial software you run on your own wallet."
- ✅ "Your funds stay in a vault only you can withdraw from."
- ✅ "A flat subscription fee — not a share of your capital or profits."
- ✅ "A market-making / liquidity strategy" (describe *what it does*, mechanically).
- ✅ "Market-making can lose money. We don't promise returns."
- ✅ "You control your keys and can withdraw at any time."
- ✅ "Transparent live telemetry — you see exactly what it's doing."

## The "custody" line (the one that matters most)

Your entire legal comfort rests on being **non-custodial software**. Protect that in
copy:

- Always frame the user as the one in control: *they* fund, *they* withdraw, *they*
  hold keys.
- The pod / operator key can **only rebalance on whitelisted pools** and can **never
  withdraw**. Say this explicitly when custody comes up.
- Never say "we hold", "we custody", "we manage", "we trade your money", "give us
  your funds". You don't and you shouldn't imply it.

---

## Risk disclosure — include it, don't bury it

Put a plain risk line anywhere you describe what the product does (site, threads,
emails). It builds trust *and* protects you:

> Market-making and liquidity provision carry risk, including loss of funds.
> Vältgeist is non-custodial software; it does not manage money or promise returns.
> Nothing here is financial advice.

Short version for tweets: *"Market-making can lose money. No returns promised."*

## About the token (F4FRD…swrm)

You've said you want to go the securities-registration route eventually — good, but
that's a legal process, not a marketing campaign. Until it's actually done:

- **Do not** market the token as an investment, a stock, or something that will
  appreciate. "Buy $VALT, it'll go up" is exactly the framing that creates
  securities-law exposure *before* you've done the paperwork.
- **Do not** tie product access or fees to holding/buying the token in your public
  copy yet — that couples the two in a way you may not want on record.
- **Fine to say** the token exists and is connected to the Euroswarms ecosystem, as
  a fact, without a value claim.
- Keep token messaging *separate* from product messaging until the legal path is
  real. When in doubt, say nothing about price.

---

## Quick gut-check before you hit post

Ask three questions:
1. **Did I promise or imply a return or safety?** → If yes, rewrite.
2. **Could someone read this as "they hold/manage my money"?** → If yes, rewrite.
3. **Am I making a value claim about the token?** → If yes, cut it.

If it passes all three, post it. When you're genuinely unsure and it's a big public
claim, that's the moment to get a real crypto/securities lawyer to glance at your
top-level site + pitch copy — a small spend that's worth it before a real launch.
